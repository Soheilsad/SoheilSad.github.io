Our Team

  • Soheil Sadabadi Founder & CEO

    Soheil believes in the ability of technology to connect people in ways that transcend borders and physical limitations.

    Ranking 10 in Iran’s national university entrance system, Soheil found his way into Harvard University at the age of 15.

    Soheil has experience working at the intersection of technology and social connections at Affable AI, Harvard IQSS, and Elysium.

    Soheil’s responsibilities include developing our vision and our technology, and he is committed to using his industry and academic experience to shape the next generation of dating apps.

  • Amira Song Founder & COO

    Amira is passionate about meaningful human connection, and believes that ‘home’ is a collection of people and memories rather than a physical location.

    Amira studied Neuroscience at Harvard, but her interests and experiences span many disciplines.

    Having competed internationally in business competitions, led the Harvard TEDx team, and worked as marketing director for two Harvard startups, Amira is ready to take Nitely to the next level.

    Amira’s responsibilities include overseeing daily operations and handling all marketing-related tasks.

  • Michael Shadpour CFO

    Michael believes that social gaming offers a unique way to build connections and meet new people.

    Michael graduated from Harvard with a degree in Chemical Biology and Computer Science. At Harvard and MIT, he was integrated into the startup ecosystem with experience as the Hult Prize Campus Director, a competitor in the MIT Sandbox Innovation Fund, and a board member of the MIT Biotech Group.

    Michael’s responsibilities at Nitely include cost and revenue forecasting, monetization, and strategic planning.

  • Andrew Cho CIO

    Andrew believes connection isn’t about how you relate before you meet — it’s about after.

    Andrew chose a quantitative approach to studying biology at Harvard. He has experience working with big data and mathematically modeling complex systems.

    Drawing on his experiences as a Harvard researcher, Andrew’s responsibilities include analyzing our data and user trends to facilitate strategic planning and organic growth.
